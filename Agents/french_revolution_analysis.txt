The major historical events that led to the French Revolution are as follows:

1. Storming of the Bastille on July 14, 1789
2. Abolition of feudal rights on August 4, 1789
3. Insurgency and popular uprising
4. Rise of Napoleon Bonaparte

These events marked significant turning points in the French Revolution and had a profound impact on the political, social, and economic landscape of France.

Conclusions:

The French Revolution was a result of a combination of factors, including political discontent, social inequality, and economic hardship. The storming of the Bastille symbolized the people's uprising against the monarchy and the beginning of the revolution. The abolition of feudal rights further fueled the revolutionary spirit and led to widespread support for change. The insurgency and popular uprising demonstrated the power of the people in demanding their rights and freedoms. Finally, the rise of Napoleon Bonaparte brought about a new era in France and reshaped the political landscape of Europe.

Overall, the French Revolution was a complex and transformative period in history, driven by a desire for liberty, equality, and justice.
